Research interests

Reproducible research; Cloud computing; Machine learning; Image processing; Neuroscience;

About me

After a BSc in Physics at Imperial and a year as a travelling salesman in Australia, I worked an analyst and software developer for 5 years, eventually running my own team. While enjoying my time in industry, I wanted to work on problems no one had solved before, so I went back to school - first to a master's in artificial intelligence at Edinburgh and then to a PhD in computational neuroscience at UCL.

Enjoying the research environment but frustrated with the slow pace, superspecialisation and precarious nature of the academic career path, I went back to industry for 3 years as an independent software development consultant. Last November, I discovered research software engineering and the Turing and now marry all my professional interests running the software engineering half of the Turing's Research Engineering team.
